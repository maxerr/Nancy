THE PRACTICE

Dr. Nancy Strauch is board certified in general adult psychiatry and is a Diplomate of the American Board of Psychiatry and Neurology. In 1987 Dr. Strauch earned her BS degree and Physician Assistant Certification from the University of TX Southwestern Medical Center in Dallas, TX. She received her medical degree from the University of TX Health Science Center, San Antonio, TX in 1992. After completing an internship in internal medicine Dr. Strauch completed her residency in general psychiatry where she served as Chief Resident. She remained at the UTHSCSA on faculty as assistant professor of psychiatry. She was medical director of the inpatient unit and attending physician in the emergency department at University Hospital. In 1999 Dr. Strauch went into private practice but continued to serve as adjunct clinical professor for the UTHSCSA medical school until her re-location to Atlanta, Georgia in 2008. Dr. Strauch has been in private practice in Atlanta since 2008 where she specializes in anxiety and mood disorders, adult ADHD and women's mental health issues. Additionally, Dr. Strauch has a wide range of experience with college student mental health serving as a consulting psychiatrist for Trinity University (San Antonio, TX), Oglethorpe University, and Clayton State University. Dr. Strauch is a member of the American Psychiatric Association, Georgia Psychiatric Physicians Association, and the Medical Association of Atlanta.
